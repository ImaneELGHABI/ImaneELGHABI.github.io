About Me

I am a data scientist with a strong passion for machine learning, AI, and decentralized systems. My work spans multiple domains, including agriculture, environmental sustainability, and voice synthesis, where I have developed innovative solutions to tackle complex problems using advanced data analysis and machine learning techniques.

Throughout my career, I have contributed to projects in diverse fields, from creating knowledge graphs in agriculture to improving energy efficiency through data science at OCP Group, and advancing voice synthesis technologies at VOSYN Inc. I am currently preparing for a PhD at the University of Amsterdam, where my research will focus on developing communication algorithms for decentralized digital twins in intelligent wastewater treatment systems.

With a solid foundation in Python, R, SQL, and machine learning frameworks such as TensorFlow, PyTorch, and Scikit-learn, I am excited to continue exploring the intersection of AI, data science, and sustainable technologies in my research and beyond.

PhD Research: Event-based Communication Algorithms for Distributed Digital Twins

My upcoming PhD research will explore event-based communication frameworks for decentralized digital twins, focusing on wastewater treatment systems. I will develop systems that support knowledge transfer between autonomous agents with varying authorization levels, leveraging AI, sensor data, and machine learning to improve the sustainability and efficiency of decentralized ecosystems.

The research is part of the "Intelligent Wastewater Treatment: Distributed Digital Twin for Clean Water (DDTclean)" project, funded by the NWO Merian Fund.
