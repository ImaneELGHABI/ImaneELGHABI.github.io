About Me

I am a data scientist with a strong background in machine learning, data analytics, and complex systems. Currently, I am preparing for my PhD at the University of Amsterdam, where I will focus on developing communication algorithms for decentralized digital twins in the field of intelligent wastewater treatment.

My expertise includes Python, R, SQL, machine learning tools (TensorFlow, Scikit-learn, PyTorch), and cloud technologies (AWS, GCP, Azure). I have previously worked on applications ranging from predictive modeling for agricultural disease detection to constructing knowledge graphs for plant research.

I am excited to combine my passion for AI, data science, and environmental sustainability as I embark on my research journey.

PhD Research: Event-based Communication Algorithms for Distributed Digital Twins

In my upcoming PhD research, I will explore the development of event-based communication frameworks for digital twins in decentralized ecosystems, specifically focusing on wastewater treatment systems. My research will aim to facilitate knowledge transfer between autonomous systems with varying levels of authorization, using AI, data analytics, and sensor data analysis to optimize performance and sustainability.

As part of the “Intelligent Wastewater Treatment: Distributed Digital Twin for Clean Water (DDTclean)” project, funded by the NWO Merian Fund, my work will directly contribute to enhancing complex infrastructures using innovative AI-driven communication systems.
