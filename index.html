About Me

I am a data scientist passionate about machine learning, AI, and decentralized systems. My journey has taken me from working on agricultural predictive models to building intelligent systems for environmental sustainability. I am preparing to embark on a PhD at the University of Amsterdam, where my research will focus on developing communication algorithms for decentralized digital twins in intelligent wastewater treatment.

With a background in Python, R, SQL, and machine learning frameworks (such as TensorFlow, PyTorch, and Scikit-learn), I am excited to contribute to the intersection of data science, AI, and sustainable technologies.

PhD Research: Event-based Communication Algorithms for Distributed Digital Twins

My upcoming PhD research will explore event-based communication frameworks for decentralized digital twins, focusing on wastewater treatment systems. I will develop systems that support knowledge transfer between autonomous agents with varying authorization levels, leveraging AI, sensor data, and machine learning to improve the sustainability and efficiency of decentralized ecosystems.

The research is part of the "Intelligent Wastewater Treatment: Distributed Digital Twin for Clean Water (DDTclean)" project, funded by the NWO Merian Fund.
