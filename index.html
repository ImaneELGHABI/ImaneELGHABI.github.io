About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Amsterdam, specializing in event-based communication algorithms for distributed digital twins. My research is part of the Intelligent Wastewater Treatment: Distributed Digital Twin for Clean Water (DDTclean) project, where I explore how digital twins can enable decentralized ecosystems to operate more efficiently and autonomously. With a foundation in data science, AI, and knowledge representation, I aim to develop solutions that integrate seamlessly into real-world infrastructure.

Research Interests

My PhD research focuses on:

  • Event-based Communication Algorithms: Developing formal models for efficient knowledge transfer between autonomous systems in decentralized wastewater treatment.
  • Distributed Knowledge Systems: Creating a framework for real-time communication between digital twins, enhancing data representation across critical infrastructures.
  • Smart Environments & Context Awareness: Investigating methods to improve interaction and decision-making within intelligent systems.
